About University

The University of Niš is a public university in Serbia. It was founded in 1965 and consists of 13 faculties with 1,492 academic staff and around 20,500 students. Since its founding, the university diploma has been acquired by more than 50,000 students, including 1,300 foreigners.

About Serbia

"Serbia is a country situated at the crossroads of Central and Southeast Europe. Its northern part lies in the southern Pannonian Plain, while the central and southern portions belong to the central Balkans. For centuries it has been a land that connects East and West, a confluence of cultures, religions, landscapes and climates. Although landlocked, Serbia is connected to distant seas via river Danube. Regarding geography and climate, it is also partly a Mediterranean country. Serbian rich historical and cultural heritage starts with prehistoric archaeological sites and its legacy from classical antiquity. Some of many mediaeval Serbian churches and monasteries are part of the UNESCO World Heritage list.”

Environment of Serbia

"Serbia is focusing on urgent environmental actions, prioritizing air quality improvements in urban areas and monitoring water resources to combat pollution. They're implementing stricter regulations and enhancing waste management practices to reduce landfill waste and pollution. Conservation efforts for ecosystems like forests and rivers are being accelerated to preserve biodiversity and promote sustainable land management. Plans for addressing environmental disasters and public awareness campaigns for sustainable living are also underway.”

LIVING COSTS

"Studying in Serbia offers excellent value for those on a budget. Renting an apartment in central Belgrade costs around 300 EUR per month, with the option to share and save money. Alternatively, student dorms offer even more affordable accommodation at around 100 EUR monthly. With additional expenses totaling around 250 EUR per month, Serbia presents an enticing opportunity to experience its culture and receive a high-quality education without breaking the bank."
